Atom habits habit

Atom habits is a book with 4 laws which:

  • treat habit as atoms, that impact on each other to form moleculars
  • works as atoms that seperately influence the process of habit forming
  • tackle 4 atomic period of the process of doing a thing, to make it a habit, or make it not a habit.

The power of atomic habit

1. Massive action vs. 1% improvement

the 1% improvement rules success is product of daily progress, and not once-in-a-life action

2. truth of progress.

3. goals and systems

Forget about the goals, focus on the systems instead

  • goals: the abstract outcome, result, like get fit
  • systems: the process that get you to the actual result

connecting with 5-step-analysis , where the goals are put forwarded first, but as a must followed by 4 other steps which refine the machine/system of accomplishing the goal.

Goals -> direction Systems -> making progress

4. atomic habits are the building block of remarkable result

5. 3 layers of bahaviour change

  1. outcome the result, the goal, the actual consequence
  2. process Machines
  3. identity role in Roles. like Athlete

6. habit loop

habit is formed with repeated small session every day, each session formed with continous feedback loop

over time, Chain formed between cue and reward

The 4 laws

1. Make it obvious

    1. Habit score card

    2. list all behaviours

    3. mark +, -, =, for positive, negative, and neutral

    1. set Time and location

    I will _behaviour_ at _time_ in _this location_. I will read at 6 p.m on my desk.

    use a cue to trigger the habit. the cue should be something you do automatically without failing.

    1. Habit stacking

    After doing _current habit_, I will _new habit_. after _brushing my teeth, I’ll strech for 5 minutes.

    1. Environment vs. Motivation

    context is the cue. think: the house full with object relations I relax on my bed. I work on my desk one space, one use

      1. make cue for good habit more easy to be seen

      place bottle in the house -> drink more place book in the house -> read more

      1. make cue for bad habits invisible

2. Make it attractive

    1. dopamine spikes
    • Dopamine spikes in craving when a drug addicter sees drug.
    • The craving causes us to take action the drug addicter uses drug after.
    1. temptation bundling

    premack’s principle: more prabable behaviors promote less prabable behaviors

    1. group influence

    into a culture where the habit is norm

    1. primitive motives -> source of craving

    the habits are modern-day solution to ancient desires

    1. reprogram the brain to accept hard habits

    Make the hard habits attractive by accosiating them with a positive experiance

    • highlight the benifit fitness = wellbeing fatigue
    1. Make bad habit unattractive

3. Make it easy

    1. habit is framed in terms of number of repetitions

    time

    1. Reducing Friction: law of least effort

    read 1 page of book every day

    1. Prime the environment for use

    Prepare the environment before.

    1. use 2-minute rule to stop procrastinating

    just, 2-minute version of the habit. target: anchor the repetition consequence: sometimes the motivation of completion arrive after the start.

    1. make bad habit impractical as possible

4. Make it satisfying

    1. The most important rule

    Satisfaction after the action promote repetition “This feels good, let’s do it for another time” What is immediately rewarded is repeated, what is immediately punished, is avoided

    1. Immediate/long-term award/punish

    adding little pleasure to long-run benefit, and pain to the long-run bad habit

    1. Rewarding yourself and feeling succesful

    Use little wins, like a progress bar, a jar of marble, paper clip,

    1. visual measurements

    logs, pagenumbers, journals…

    1. Habit track
    1. when bad days come……

    Don't break the chain! do the small version, just don’t break the chain.

    1. Habit contract.

    claims clear that you will honor a particular habit and that there will be punishments if you do not

Method

  1. set target HABIT, and GOOD? to signify if it’s a good habit.
  2. for each phase of habit loop, take corresponding action to push the habit to a direction of:
    • obvious - invisible (for cue)
    • attractive - unattractive (for craving)
    • easy - hard (for responsing)
    • satisfying/pleasure - unsatisfying/painful (for reward)
  3. Set mindset(background sound/principle/belief/viewpoint)
    • like, when developing work out habit:
      • thinking that 1% improvement rules
      • thinking that showing up’s half the bottle
      • thinking that from the 3 layers, I identity myself as an athelete
      • knowing that I’m probably in the valley of disappointment, so be patient. it’s not a decision-result correspondency, but a viewpoint with respect of fact. It’s a cognition, a thinking and evaluation with no interacting with the world, not changing the facts, no engineering, but by making evaluation of myself to certain realistic standards, I gain a cirtain emotion mode, which impact my feeling, action, motivation, crave, and reward(satisfacition.)
